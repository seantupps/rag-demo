Software is an integral component of Tesla's business model, enabling various functionalities and revenue streams:

*   **Over-the-Air Updates and Upgrades**: Tesla vehicles are capable of remote over-the-air updates, allowing customers to purchase additional paid options and features through the Tesla app or in-vehicle interface. This also facilitates offering certain options and features on a subscription basis [ITEM 1. BUSINESS]. Over-the-air software updates are provisioned upon vehicle transfer and recognized as revenue over the expected ownership life of the vehicle [ITEM 8. FINANCIAL STATEMENTS AND SUPPLEMENTARY DATA].
*   **Subscription Services**: Customers can purchase subscriptions for features such as FSD (Supervised) and premium connectivity after vehicle delivery. Revenue from these subscriptions is recognized either over time or at a point in time, depending on contractual terms [ITEM 8. FINANCIAL STATEMENTS AND SUPPLEMENTARY DATA].
*   **FSD (Supervised) Features**: Revenue related to FSD (Supervised) features is recognized when functionality is delivered to the customer, with ongoing maintenance recognized over time [ITEM 8. FINANCIAL STATEMENTS AND SUPPLEMENTARY DATA].
*   **Remote Diagnostics and Service**: Vehicle connectivity, enabled by software, allows Tesla to diagnose and remedy many problems remotely and proactively, enhancing service efficiency [ITEM 1. BUSINESS].
*   **Supercharger Network Support**: Tesla remains responsible for software updates, network operations, maintenance, and driver support for its Supercharger network, including those purchased and installed by third parties through the Supercharger for Business program [ITEM 1. BUSINESS].
